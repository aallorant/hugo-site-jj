Alongside her sculpture and works on paper, Jeanne Jozon explored the decorative arts: pyrography on wood, hand-crafted toys produced during the Great War, vases and ewers in stoneware and bronze for the manufactures of Sèvres and Mehun-sur-Yèvre, salt cellars and inkwells in pewter. These pieces mark her closeness to the Art Nouveau movement and her taste for the artisanal handling of materials.
