Jeanne Jozon's œuvre spans almost half a century — from her first plaster statuettes in the mid-1890s through the bronzes of her final years on the eve of the Second World War. It unfolds across four principal worlds: sculpture, which remained her centre of gravity; Art Nouveau jewelry; works on paper (drawings, sketches, and pastels); and decorative arts (vases, toys, pyrography).
